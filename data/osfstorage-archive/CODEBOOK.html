CCF Database Codebook

Complete Annotation Framework

This codebook provides operational definitions for all 67 annotation categories in the CCF Database.

Note: Of the 67 categories, 63 are fully available, 2 are partially available (security subcategories with limited training data in some languages, marked with †), and 2 are excluded from analysis (health subcategories with insufficient training data, marked with *).

Thematic Frames

Economic Frame

# Category Code Definition
1 Economic Frame (Primary) economic_frame If the sentence refers to climate change as an economic issue, including its negative or positive impacts on the economy, the financial costs or benefits of climate action, or the carbon footprint of the economic or industrial sector.
2 Negative impacts on economy eco_neg_impact If the sentence refers to the negative effects of climate change on the economy, such as damage to infrastructure, loss of agricultural productivity, disruptions to trade, increased costs for businesses, or economic decline.
3 Positive impacts on economy eco_pos_impact If the sentence refers to the positive effects of climate change on the economy, such as new agricultural opportunities, opening of new trade routes, or potential for economic growth.
4 Economic costs of action eco_cost If the sentence refers to the negative economic impacts caused by climate policies, such as costs of transitioning to green energy, investments in adaptation, or job losses in fossil fuel industries.
5 Economic benefits of action eco_benefit If the sentence refers to the positive economic impacts from climate policies, such as job creation in renewable energy, investments in green technologies, or cost savings from efficiency.
6 Economic sector footprint eco_footprint If the sentence refers to the carbon footprint of economic/industrial sectors, including emissions from manufacturing, transportation, energy production, mining, or agriculture.

Health Frame

# Category Code Definition
7 Health Frame (Primary) health_frame If the sentence refers to climate change as a public health issue, including impacts on physical or mental health, health co-benefits of climate action, or the carbon footprint of the health sector.
8 Negative health impacts health_neg_impact If the sentence refers to negative effects of climate change on human health, such as infectious diseases, respiratory illnesses, heat-related illnesses, or mental health disorders.
9 *Positive health impacts health_pos_impact If the sentence refers to positive effects of climate change on human health, such as reduced cold-related mortality or improved growing seasons for food.
10 Health co-benefits health_cobenefit If the sentence refers to health benefits from climate policies, such as improved air quality, better nutrition, or reduced respiratory diseases.
11 *Health sector footprint health_footprint If the sentence refers to the carbon footprint of the health and medical sector, including hospital operations, pharmaceutical production, or medical waste.

Security Frame

# Category Code Definition
12 Security Frame (Primary) security_frame If the sentence refers to effects of climate change on security, such as violent conflict, climate refugees, disruption of military operations, or the carbon footprint of the defense sector.
13 Climate refugees security_refugees If the sentence refers to displacement of populations or climate refugees as a result of natural disasters or environmental degradation.
14 Resource conflict security_conflict If the sentence refers to conflicts over natural resources due to climate change, such as disputes over water, land, or energy resources.
15 †Military assistance security_military If the sentence refers to deployment of military forces after a natural disaster.
16 †Military disruption security_disruption If the sentence refers to disruption of military operations due to natural disasters affecting military bases.

Justice Frame

# Category Code Definition
17 Justice Frame (Primary) justice_frame If the sentence frames climate change as a justice or moral issue, including winners/losers, differentiated responsibilities, unequal impacts, or intergenerational concerns.
18 Winners and losers justice_winners If the sentence refers to how climate policies create winners and losers, benefiting some groups while disadvantaging others.
19 Differentiated responsibility justice_responsibility If the sentence refers to unequal responsibility for causing climate change, especially the principle of common but differentiated responsibilities.
20 Unequal vulnerability justice_vulnerability If the sentence highlights how different populations are unequally affected by climate impacts due to geographic, social, or economic vulnerabilities.
21 Unequal access to action justice_access If the sentence refers to unequal capacity to act on climate change due to financial, technical, or institutional constraints.
22 Intergenerational justice justice_intergen If the sentence refers to intergenerational justice, meaning current decisions should not compromise the rights of future generations.

Political Frame

# Category Code Definition
23 Political Frame (Primary) political_frame If the sentence frames climate change as a political issue, including policy adoption, political debates, positioning by parties/leaders, or public opinion data.
24 Policy action pol_action If the sentence refers to formal approval or enactment of climate policies at any level of government.
25 Political debate pol_debate If the sentence refers to political debates about climate policies, effectiveness of actions, or government responsibilities.
26 Political positioning pol_position If the sentence refers to explicit stances of politicians or parties on climate policies.
27 Public opinion data pol_opinion If the sentence refers to surveys, polls, or studies measuring public attitudes toward climate change.

Scientific Frame

# Category Code Definition
28 Scientific Frame (Primary) scientific_frame If the sentence frames climate change as a scientific issue, including research, discoveries, consensus, or controversy.
29 Scientific debate sci_debate If the sentence refers to debates within the scientific community about climate science or technologies.
30 Scientific discovery sci_discovery If the sentence explains climate science mechanisms or presents scientific discoveries.
31 Climate skepticism sci_skepticism If the sentence questions the validity, accuracy, or sufficiency of climate science.
32 Science defense sci_defense If the sentence defends the robustness or credibility of climate science in response to skepticism.

Environmental Frame

# Category Code Definition
33 Environmental Frame (Primary) environmental_frame If the sentence frames climate change as an environmental issue, including habitat degradation, biodiversity loss, or impacts on fauna/flora.
34 Habitat loss env_habitat If the sentence refers to long-term or irreversible disappearance of natural environments due to climate change.
35 Species loss env_species If the sentence refers to negative impacts on animal and plant species, including habitat changes, mortality, or biodiversity loss.

Cultural Frame

# Category Code Definition
36 Cultural Frame (Primary) cultural_frame If the sentence frames climate change as a cultural issue, including artistic depictions, event disruptions, Indigenous practices, or cultural sector footprint.
37 Artistic representation cult_art If the sentence refers to cultural representations of climate change in art, literature, music, or film.
38 Event disruption cult_event_impact If the sentence refers to how climate disrupts cultural or sports events.
39 Indigenous practices cult_indigenous If the sentence refers to erosion of Indigenous cultural practices due to climate change.
40 Cultural footprint cult_footprint If the sentence refers to the carbon footprint of cultural, artistic, or sports sectors.

Actors/Messengers

# Category Code Definition
41 Messenger (Primary) messenger If the sentence includes mentions or quotes of persons or organizations by journalists.
42 Health expert msg_health If the quoted statement comes from someone with medical or health expertise.
43 Economic expert msg_economic If the quoted statement comes from someone with economic or financial expertise.
44 Security expert msg_security If the quoted statement comes from someone with security or defense expertise.
45 Legal expert msg_legal If the quoted statement comes from someone with legal expertise.
46 Cultural expert msg_cultural If the quoted statement comes from someone with cultural, artistic, or sports expertise.
47 Natural scientist msg_scientist If the quoted statement comes from someone with natural science expertise.
48 Social scientist msg_social If the quoted statement comes from someone with social science expertise.
49 Activist msg_activist If the quoted statement comes from an activist or advocacy organization.
50 Public official msg_official If the quoted statement comes from a politician or government representative.

Events

# Category Code Definition
51 Event (Primary) event If the sentence refers to the occurrence of events (weather, political, meetings, publications, cultural, protests).
52 Extreme weather evt_weather If the sentence refers to extreme meteorological events causing disruption or harm.
53 Meeting evt_meeting If the sentence refers to conferences, summits, or official meetings.
54 Publication evt_publication If the sentence refers to release of reports, studies, or articles.
55 Election evt_election If the sentence refers to elections at any level.
56 Policy announcement evt_policy If the sentence refers to announcement of policies or strategies.
57 Judiciary decision evt_judiciary If the sentence refers to court rulings or legal proceedings.
58 Cultural event evt_cultural If the sentence refers to cultural or sports events.
59 Protest evt_protest If the sentence refers to demonstrations or protests.

Solutions

# Category Code Definition
60 Solution (Primary) solution If the sentence refers to solutions addressing climate change through mitigation or adaptation.
61 Mitigation sol_mitigation If the sentence refers to strategies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
62 Adaptation sol_adaptation If the sentence refers to strategies to manage climate impacts and enhance resilience.

Other Annotations

# Category Code Definition
63 Positive tone tone_positive If the tone is optimistic, reassuring, or enthusiastic.
64 Negative tone tone_negative If the tone is alarming, critical, or pessimistic.
65 Neutral tone tone_neutral If the tone is informative or descriptive without strong emotion.
66 Canadian context canada If the sentence refers to Canada in any context.
67 Urgency urgency If the sentence conveys urgency or alarmism about climate action.

Named Entity Recognition

The ner_entities column contains JSON-formatted named entities:

{
  "PER": ["Justin Trudeau", "Greta Thunberg"],
  "ORG": ["Environment Canada", "IPCC"],
  "LOC": ["Alberta", "Paris"]
}

Models Used

Language Entity Type Model
English PER, ORG, LOC BERT-base-NER
French PER spaCy fr_core_news_lg
French ORG, LOC CamemBERT-NER

Data Types

All annotation columns are binary integers:

  • 1 = Category present
  • 0 = Category absent

Notes

  • Categories marked with * are excluded from analysis due to insufficient training data
  • Categories marked with † are partially available (limited training data in some languages)
  • 67 categories total: 63 fully available, 2 partially available (†), 2 excluded (*)
  • Sentence text is excluded for copyright compliance
  • All definitions are from the peer-reviewed methodology paper
